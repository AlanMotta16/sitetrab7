DISCOVER VIDEOGAME CONSOLES

If you like videogames, then you probably own a videogame console. They have almost as much power as PCs, but for a much better price. And they come with their exclusive games. Get to know the big 3 of consoles.

XBOX

Xbox is a videogame console, that you probably recognize the name of. It is made and owned by Microsoft, but is its own brand. It's in a constant fight to be the top console in the world (against the Playstation). Here are the Xbox consoles that have existed:
*Xbox - Also known as the 'original Xbox', was released in 2001, and did really well.
*Xbox 360 - The one a lot of us probably had growing up. Had some major problems, but still is iconic. Released in 2005.
*Xbox One - Ouch, this one wasn't great. Released in 2013, was poorly managed, until they released the S and X versions, which were great.
*Xbox Seres X|S - The MOST POWERFUL console ever (at least the Series X). Released in 2020, is the current generation of consoles.

PLAYSTATION

Playstation is one of the original videogame consoles, back in the 90's, with the PS1. Regarded as "the one to beat" in the console wars. Its owned by Sony, which helps with recognition. It has a lot of iconic games, that you'll see here. Here are its consoles:
*PS1 - The OG, is iconic as it gets when we're talking about videogames. Released all the way back in 1994.
*PS2 - Released in 2000, this is the one we compare to the original Xbox, and a lot of people love this one.
*PS3 - The one that lost the generation the 360, was released in 2005, and had nothing remarkable about it.
*PS4 - OK, this one beat the Xbox One out of the water, also releasing in 2013, and being super awesome in a lot of ways.
*PS5 - Released in 2020 (alongside the Xbox Series X|S), is the current generation for Playstation.

NINTENDO

Nintendo, along with Playstation, have existed pretty much since the beginning. But now, Nintendo isn't really competing with Sony and Microsoft's consoles, they're in a league of their own. They have had so many consoles, both for home and handheld, the biggest probably being the Wii (remember that one?). These are their highlights:
*NES - The first one, released in 1983 (ok this is really old).
*SNES -Released in 1990, stands for Super Nintendo Entertainment System.
*Nintendo 64 - With the 64 being for 64-bits, it was revolutionary. Released in 1996.
*Nintendo GameCube - A lot of these just stuck in their time, especially the GameCube, released in 2001.
*Wii - This is the one everybody remembers (like the Xbox 360). Used motion controls. Released in 2006.
*WiiU - Released in 2012, the not-so-successful successor to the Wii also used the motion controls, but just pretty much died.
*Nintendo Switch - Even though it released in 2017, is the current generation and compared to the Xbox Series X|S and the PS5.
